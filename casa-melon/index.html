Costa del Sol Oriental

Nerja — Jewel of Andalucia

Nestled between the Sierra Almijara mountains and the shimmering Mediterranean, Nerja is one of southern Spain's most beloved coastal towns. Whitewashed buildings, cobbled lanes, the iconic Balcon de Europa, and over 300 days of sunshine a year — this is Andalucia at its most enchanting.

320 Days of Sun

One of the most enviable climates in Europe, with warm summers and mild winters — perfect for year-round visits.

Pristine Beaches

From the lively Playa de Burriana to the secluded coves of Maro, Nerja's beaches are among the finest on the Costa del Sol.

Rich History

Home to the world-famous prehistoric Cuevas de Nerja, the town blends Moorish heritage with authentic Andalusian charm.

Exceptional Food

Fresh seafood, traditional tapas, and local Axarquia wines make Nerja a destination for passionate food lovers.

"The best-kept secret of the Costa del Sol — where authentic Andalusian life meets the brilliance of the sea."

Things to Do

Explore the Area

From prehistoric caves to hidden coves and hilltop villages, there is no shortage of wonders within easy reach of Casa Melon.

Must See
Landmark

Balcon de Europa

The iconic palm-lined promenade jutting into the sea is the beating heart of Nerja and one of Spain's most photographed viewpoints. Watch the sun set over the Mediterranean from this magical terrace surrounded by Moorish watchtowers.

5 min walk Free
UNESCO Heritage
Culture & History

Cuevas de Nerja

Discovered in 1959, these breathtaking prehistoric caves contain the world's largest cave column and spectacular stalactite formations stretching 4,800 years of history. A world-class natural wonder just minutes from the apartment.

5 min drive Ticketed
Local Favourite
Beach

Playa de Burriana

Nerja's longest and most vibrant beach, with crystal-clear waters, watersports, and the legendary Ayo's beach restaurant — home to the world's biggest paella pan cooked over an open fire on the sand.

15 min walk Free
Day Trip

Frigiliana

Just 5km into the mountains, this perfectly preserved Moorish village is one of Andalucia's most beautiful pueblos blancos. Cobbled lanes draped with flower pots, artisan honey and wine shops, and sweeping valley views.

10 min drive Free

Rio Chillar Walk

One of Andalucia's most spectacular hikes — wade upstream through a dramatic river gorge carved into towering limestone walls. Cool, refreshing, and completely unlike anything else. An unforgettable 3–4 hour round trip.

3–4 hours Free

Where to Eat

Taste of Nerja

From legendary beach paellas to intimate tapas bars and fresh catch of the day, Nerja's dining scene is one of the great pleasures of a stay here.

Iconic Institution

Ayo's Restaurant

No visit to Nerja is complete without experiencing Ayo's legendary beachside paella on Playa de Burriana. The image of Ayo himself cooking over an enormous open wood fire has become synonymous with the town — vast paella pans feeding hundreds of diners under the sun. Arrive before noon or you will miss out on this once-in-a-lifetime dining experience.

Dining Tips for Nerja

Eat Spanish Hours

Lunch is the main meal — served 2pm to 4pm. Dinner rarely starts before 9pm. Embrace the rhythm and you will eat far better.

Order a Cana

A small draft beer (cana) is the perfect companion to tapas and costs around €1.50 — one of life's great bargains.

Order the Catch

Always ask what fish came in today. Espetos — sardines on a bamboo skewer, grilled over an open fire — are an absolute must.

Book in Summer

July and August are busy. Book popular restaurants at least a day or two ahead to avoid disappointment at your first choice.

Travel Information

Getting Here

Nerja is easy to reach from the UK and across Europe, making it the perfect destination for a long weekend or a proper holiday in the sun.

By Air

Fly into Malaga Airport (AGP) — one of Spain's busiest airports with direct flights from most UK cities. easyJet, Ryanair, Jet2, and British Airways all serve the route. The airport is just 55km west of Nerja.

Airport to Nerja

A private transfer takes around 50–60 minutes and is very reasonably priced. Car hire from the airport offers the most flexibility — the A-7 coastal road to Nerja is a stunning drive along the Costa del Sol with sea views the whole way.

By Bus

Regular ALSA coach services run directly from Malaga bus station to Nerja, with a journey time of around 1.5 hours. Buses also connect Nerja to Granada (2.5 hours), making a combined trip very easy to plan.

Where We Are

Nerja sits on the Costa del Sol Oriental, 52km east of Malaga and 110km west of Almeria. The town is also within easy reach of Granada (2 hours) and the beautiful Axarquia mountain villages.
